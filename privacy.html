Permissions Explained

Reality requires certain permissions to function. Here's exactly why each is needed:

  • Accessibility Service: Required to detect which app is currently open and block access to restricted apps. This data is processed locally and never stored or transmitted.
  • Usage Stats Access: Required to track your screen time. This data is only displayed to you and stored locally on your device.
  • Display Over Other Apps: Required to show warning overlays when you try to open a blocked app.
  • Device Administrator (Optional): Only needed if you enable anti-uninstall protection. Can be disabled anytime.

üîí Important Note

Reality does NOT have Internet permission. This means even if we wanted to collect data (we don't), it would be technically impossible to send it anywhere.

Local Data Storage

Reality stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Your blocked apps list
  • Your blocked websites list
  • Focus mode settings and schedules
  • Bedtime mode settings
  • App usage statistics (for your viewing only)
  • Strict mode settings

This data is stored in the app's private storage and is automatically deleted when you uninstall the app. No backups are made to any cloud service.
